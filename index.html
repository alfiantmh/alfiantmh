Hospital Treatment

Medications

Hospitals may prescribe various medications to treat gastric diseases. These can include proton pump inhibitors (PPIs) to reduce stomach acid production, antibiotics to treat Helicobacter pylori infection, antacids to neutralize stomach acid, or medications to manage symptoms such as nausea or pain.

Read More

Endoscopy

Endoscopy is a procedure performed in a hospital setting that involves inserting a flexible tube with a camera (endoscope) into the digestive tract. It allows doctors to visualize the stomach and diagnose or treat certain conditions, such as peptic ulcers, gastritis, or tumors. Endoscopy can also be used for procedures like biopsy or removing polyps.

Read More

Surgery

In some cases, surgical intervention may be necessary to treat certain gastric diseases. Surgical procedures can range from minimally invasive techniques, such as laparoscopic surgery, to more extensive procedures like gastric bypass surgery for obesity-related conditions. Surgery may be performed to remove tumors, repair ulcers, or address other underlying issues.

Read More

Monitoring and Follow-up

Hospitals provide close monitoring of patients with gastric diseases to track their progress, manage symptoms, and adjust treatment plans as needed. Follow-up appointments may be scheduled to assess the effectiveness of the treatment, monitor any potential complications, and make necessary adjustments.
